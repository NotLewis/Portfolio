Optimisation and Performance

Last Updated: 10th November 2018

Written by Lewis Hall

The moment has finally arrived, you have finished that website you have worked so hard on for what seems like years. All those late nights and stress was worth it.

But wait! You are not finished yet! You need to make sure it is all optimized!

But Lewis, there are so tools and techniques, which should I use? Well, look no further, in this blog post I will show you the best tools you should use for performance and optimisation.

Code

Making sure your code is validated is essential when checking your websites optimisation. The easiest method of checking your HTML code is validated is by using the handy tool that can be found on the W3C Markup Validation Serivce website. You have the option to validate through your URL, uploading your HTML file or even validating by directly inputting your HTML code. If your code isn’t validated properly you will get a series of messages telling you what the problem is and how you can fix it.

And don’t worry, there is a CSS Validator too that follows the same premise.

Optimisation

Now that your code if fully validated and there are no problems it’s time for you to check your websites optimisation. “Website optimization is important because it helps your website visitors be more successful with their visits to your website.” (Qualaroo,Unknow:online)

The tools I recommend for optimisation are PageSpeed Inisght by Google and Site checker pro to check how quickly your website loads. It provides you with suggestions on what you could change to make your website perform better.

And if you haven’t already I highly recommend that you go back and check the optimization on your images. This can easily be done through online programmes such as Tiny PNG where you simply upload your image file and it outputs a smaller file size. Do this with all your images to make sure that your website loads as fast as possible.

Search Engine Optimisation (SEO)

Not only does SEO “increase the quantity and quality of traffic to your website through organic search engine results” (Moz,Unknow:online)it also helps improve the user experience and usability of a website. A quick way to check your websites SEO is to use Site Checker Pro

To help improve your SEO you should check that you have included the following into your website.

  • Meta descriptions – This is the short paragraph of text that appears under your page in the search results. Depending on the detail of this may detour potential visitors on visiting your site.
  • Title tags – This is a brief way search engines see what your site is about.
  • Alt tags – This is the most common feature that most people forget when they upload images onto their website. Not only do descriptive alt tags help in search results, it is also very beneficial to those that may be using screen readers and want to know what the image is.
